# Decision journal — 52


## Decision
Root cause confirmed: The regex `/\s*[^.!?\s][^.!?]*[.!?]?/g` silently drops consecutive punctuation characters. Two sub-problems: (1) `[^.!?\s]` requires every segment to begin with a non-delimiter character, so delimiter-only runs like `..` or `!` after a sentence fail to match and are skipped; (2) `[.!?]?` captures at most one trailing delimiter, so `...` or `?!` lose everything after the first delimiter.

Fix approach (single regex change): Replace `[.!?]?` with `[.!?]*` AND add an alternation for delimiter-only segments. The corrected regex: `/\s*(?:[.!?]+|[^.!?\s][^.!?]*[.!?]*)/g`. The `[.!?]+` alternative is tried first to capture runs of consecutive delimiters (with optional leading whitespace from the outer `\s*`). The second alternative captures normal text segments with greedy trailing delimiter capture. This preserves the exact character-level reconstruction guarantee.

No signature changes, no algorithm changes — purely a regex fix. The only caller is `prepareForSynthesis` in the same lib directory.
